About Me

I’m Jishnu, a Mechanical Engineering student driven by a deep curiosity for how things work—and how they can work better. I thrive at the intersection of design, materials, and intelligent systems, where engineering meets innovation. My journey spans diverse domains: from designing a structurally optimized motorcycle swingarm and developing metal matrix composites, to pioneering 3D-printed ceramics and leading the Motomanipal team to build high-performance electric bikes. At HAL, I applied machine learning for predictive maintenance through Health Usage and Monitoring Systems. At Wayve Mobility, I led the mechanical design of an urban electric two-wheeler from the ground up. Beyond the technical, I take pride in leading collaborative teams, navigating ambiguity, and learning relentlessly. Whether tackling aerospace structures or exploring how AI can transform engineering, I’m guided by a passion to keep evolving—and to lead others in doing the same.
